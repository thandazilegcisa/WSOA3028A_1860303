An introduction to Sophiatown

An Ode to the Sound of freedom


Despite all the struggle, Sophiatown you made Jazz your Home

In the 80s and early 90s Jazz was used to fight oppression.
Thus, residents of Sophiatown often cultivated a rich culture based on using shebeens as public spaces where people could gather for entertainment.

This as a result played a significant role in shaping and developing Jazz in South Africa. Created from a need to survive together. Therefore, Sophiatown residents were determined to lead respectable lives in the shadow of a state that was hostile toward the residents. The musical King Kong, a musical festival. Is described by historians as the reason behind the blossoming of Sophiatown's multi-racial cultural exploits in the 1950s. The musical depicted street life, violence, disparity of life and jazz. When King Kong premiered in Johannesburg a 70 strong cast that featured Hugh Masekela, Miriam Makeba, Gwigwi Mrwebi and Phyllis Mnqoma became an overnight hit.

These are some of those faces

However as life continued. On 9 February 1955, the apartheid government rolled out the demolition of Sophiatown and forceably removed 60000 of its inhabitants

Thats is not the end of it though.
Because although the physical materiality of Sophiatown was demolished i.e buildings houses. The cultural artefacts including literature and theatre plays signified the time in which they were living in which is an artists solem duty.

The influences of Sophiatown live on in jazz till to day. And that is the beauty behind the madness.
