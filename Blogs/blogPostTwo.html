A reflection on Tim Berners Lee's: The World Wide Web.

Thandazile Gcisa
March 18 2021

"The world wide web first began life at CERN, a European laboratory for Particle Physics in Geneva, Switzerland".
(Berners-Lee, 1994)

Berners- Lee, the inventor of the concept of the web, came up with a solution to aid the dissemination of research and information amongst his peers and the critical mass. As such he came up with a distributed network that allows researchers from different parts of the world to organize and efficiently gather information from multiple sources.

The web was designed, with the idea of creating a distributed hypertext system that affords users with the ability to work independently. This design is reflected and illustrated by version control systems, namely git and git hub, which provides a platform or server within which a team member can independently work on a team project from remote locations around the world.

Typically a client (browser) is used to request information off a server (a computer that runs the back-end of the internet) which, is used to store the metadata of any data that is represented on the internet. The server then responds to the clients request by retrieving an html document, which it interprets and describes to the client in order to render multimedia information.

What is multimedia information? Multimedia information is information that can be rendered as text, visuals and/ or audio. How is this reflected in my site? . Well the character of the site uses text, images and videos to describe and illustrate an account of history, and this kind of media is not special only to this site but also Whatsapp, Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram uses multimedia to render information. Therefore, this is the reason why such websites are able to provide or mediate cultural experiences because a large part of our lives in today's society adopts teachings and information form these sources and therefore this characterises the internet as an intersection of different practices, traditions and knowledge that enables the globalization of culture and beliefs. This might be good or bad. It might be good in the sense that it allows people to form or be a part of a cultural identity, but it might be bad because it could create intercultural conflict where beliefs are contested, for example the various big technological companies are banned in China because as we probably all know by know China is an authoritarian country so it does not want its people to bare witness to anything that might negate their own culture. Therefore as Uncle Ben always says "With great power comes great responsibility" so it is how we use the internet that is important to always consider.
