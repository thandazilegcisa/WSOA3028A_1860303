Towards a study of information geographies: (im)mutable augmentations and a mapping of the geographies of information

Thandazile Gcisa
12 May 2021

Information is innately entrenched within the context within which it is formed and argued upon. Therefore, the study of information geographies examine how context shapes or is mediated by the infrastructure, technologies and networks within a specific context. Ever since the proliferation of post-mechanical re-production ability of computers the transmission and movement of information has become much more accessibile, and by accessibile I mean non-exclusive. However, there are are certain barriers to entry that have their own geographic effects on the overall reception of information across the world, this includes considerations regarding how, easily certain geographies have access to participate and represent themselves on the internet using computing devices such as mobile phones and computers.

Hence, this website aims to, represent the global south in that regard because our character is a cultural historian that seeks to document his love for jazz music and how the space within which the jazz was made influenced its output. Therefore, by so doing the character represents those who were previously not represented and searches for the beaut of that representation to highligh and use this website as a platform to share (participate) and represent the story of where he comes from. The technical component of the site is also indicative of this sentiment because this site is hosted on github pages and therefore as a result counts as another commit, push of information every time i execute those git functions.

Before, the impetus of the internet the knowledge economy of information was unchanging over a period of time. Due to the traditional printing of information on paper, which meant that information was simultaneously fixed to a physical object and its local context. However, today information is not fixed to a physical object, because the internet as a platform, this site mediates and links research from all around the world, although the problem with this new affordance is that accessing the technology itself has now become the priviledge the power that gives people not only the power to participate but also encode how they want to be represented on the internet, hence they shape information rather than just reflecting and decoding information.

This is where the concept of power and its relation to information geographies becomes important to understand because power hinges on the ability for every citizen to be an agent that has agency over the internet as a platform to attain, distribute, and create information. Otherwise if the democratisation of information is not possible a ruling or dominant "threshold guardian or gatekeeper" would control how certain geographies are represented without consent from the actual context the information is from. Therefore, to investigate how, where and by whom is information created by we need to investigate how different geographies access, participate and represent themselves on the internet.

South Africa (which is where my character and i resides) much like in the rest of africa, internet penetration is relatively low compared to the rest of the world (Global North). Coupled with high broadband costs in some areas in the continent, historical socio-economic disadvantages also play a major role in who has the power to access the internet because the higher the economic cost of access on the internet does not bode well with the issue of low distributions of wealth and income in the global south. For example in a country like South Africa where a group of market competitors (MTN, Vodacom, Cell C, Telkom) control the market the price of data could take up to a two thirds of the salary of an average worker, which makes access unequal because not all branches of society have the power to participate.

With this in mind the according to Graham et al (2015) to understand participation he explores three avenues, namely, domain names, github users and wikipedia edits and for the sake of this article i'll focus on github users. Mostly because this sites domain name and code base is hosted on github. Despite being the largest hosting service in the world and providing a platform for a decentralised distribution of information the relation between internet users and github users is substantial, at least in the global south. The Middle East, North Africa and Sub-Saharan africa stand out with the lowest levels of participation or "contribution" to the worlds largest code base, combined according to Graham et al (2015) the regions are home to less than 1% of github users and commits. Therefore in sum this indicates where the power of participation, and representation lies. However this site in indicative of the fact that the global south has the ability, power and agency to represent themselves if given access as i have been given this laptop that i am typing by providing access through institutions of learning,and libraries etc.

Grahm, M., De Sabbata, S., Zook, A. (2015). Towards a study of information geographies: (im)mutable augmentations and a mapping of the geographies of information. Geography and Environment. University of Oxford.
