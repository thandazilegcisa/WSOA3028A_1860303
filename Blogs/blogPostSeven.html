Interpreting the Layout of Web Pages

Thandazile Gcisa
8 April 2021

Ever since the proliferation of the internet interpreting the layout of web pages has become something that users innately do.And this is largely due to the fact that as stated in previous articles, the internet is an extension of our daily lives, and our daily lives often includes reading a book, newspaper or even shopping newspaper. Therefore the architecture of web pages are not outliers in this regard, because they employ the same strategies that other textual mediums exhibit.

In fact from a historical perspective, news and shopping web sites are relative newcomers to well-established fields with long-standing practices and conventions. Hence, since the proliferation of the internet web pages have adopted characteristic document formats and designs based on what has proven to work well for print media. Therefore, much like print media the use of multiple and modular layouts have been used to either replicate a news platform on the internet or simply to effectively manage an abundance of information on the page if any. For years multi-column layouts have been used in news and shopping newspapers, mainly because they make pages easier to read and provide flexibility for the way in which layout is designed On the other hand modular layouts, commonly used by newspapers and magazines - are even more flexible than strict column formats. They use a combination of vertical an horizontal shapes to arrange the information.

As such following convention is somewhat useful because like in the case of books or newspapers. Generally people read from the top-left corner to the bottom-right (except if you are from Japan and read from right to left). And by virtue of following conventions with regard to core structural functions such as navigation and format of content is important because it can improve the accessibility of a platform. If a website (as mentioned in previous blogs) is accessible it means that assistive technologies such as screen readers would be a able to assist visually impaired people. However in a study that was done by the authour it is shown that web sites that display a modular layout are the most problematic for visually impaired users because although they might be easier and and prettier to read for sighted users, the same cannot be said for visually impaired users because its complexity poses a threat to to the ability of screen readers to efficiently communicate the embedded meanings of the document.
