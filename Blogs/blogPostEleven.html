User Experience - A Research Agenda (Hassenzahl et al.)

Thandazile Gcisa
28 April 2021

In the paper of the above title, authours Hassenzahl et al. (2005) examine what is meant by user experience. I also attempts to demonstrate the relation or lack thereof of the designer's intentions and a user's actual experience. Since the beginning of the proliferation or web apps, games and the internet interaction on these applications or mediums have called for considering how a user will experience using the medium. Thus, early research on the phenomenological experience of products have largely focused on the outcome of goals in work settings. This includes leader-boards, badges, point-systems and UI to ensure that a product is designed for an optimal experience.

Hence, early research largely focused on reward based gratification methods for constructing "enjoyable" user experiences. Despite the fact that this might be good for short-term results focus hardly ever shifted to creating intrinsic motivation or value rather than extrinsic motivation. Intrinsic motivation becomes very important when considering the implications of user experiences because it means that users are motivated to experience the product not because of a reward but for personal, transformational reasons in order to satisfy general human needs. As such Hassenzahl et al. (2005) argues that "future human computer interactions must be concerned about the pragmatic needs of interactive products such as identification and evocation". Which is important because intrinsic motivation becomes and instrument in providing agency for the user to use and experience the product continuously.

In the paper authours highlight two ways in which User Experience can be achieved namely through emotional and affective design, and experientially. Although for the sake of this article I'll focus on experientially mainly because a designer designs for emotion and not emotion itself so it would be difficult to quantify if the emotions in questions is or can be achieved. The experiential perspective on UX emphasises two aspects of technological use namely, situatedness and temporality.From this perspective "an experience is a unique combination of various elements, such as the product and the internal states of the user which extend over time with a definitive beginning and end." I completely agree with this statement because how designer should approach designing products is to design to meet a users needs, coupled by considering how accessible and usable the product is and if it is able to provide the user with positive feedback. For example in this site as of assignment three when a user hovers over a clickable link the link changes colour and background colour This way the user is able to know if their actions are inputted by the system or not which provides for user experiences. And the extent to which this sites character is fleshed out (including the current theory) is also meant to provide intrinsic value to the site and give the site meaning as to why it should exist and why users should care.
