Reflection on a Twitter Post

Thandazile Gcisa
June 2021

I think that this statement is very true an that it also represents the fact that our societies socio-economic structures have also been homogenised by more often than not males, and more specifically white males. As such because of the fact that this marginalisation is also present in institutions of power (Educational or Enterprises) it illustrates a stark picture of the digital divide in the use, access and representation of the internet. This is significant because as discussed in previous blog posts because "the medium is the message" and if women cannot use, access and represent themselves on the internet they cannot transport, share and inscribe their message within the internet. This is especially true for black women because black women are marginalised not only from the object of their body, but their race, and so this dual marginalisation sets them back two-fold. So Despite the statement being true possibly, the majority of the 10% of women that can use, access and represent themselves on platforms like wikipedia are white women from the global north. Most women from the global south are marginalised to the extent of access and not only representation and participation on wikipedia.

Which is why the internet needs African feminist's because for example Sheena Magenya states that "online gender-based violence has been of paramount concern raised all over the world by women, LGBTIQA and non-gender conforming people, but legislation that recognises and acts on these attacks and violence is largely absent in many african countries." This is true because most african constitutions are not progressive as that of South Africa for example, most laws (an institution of power) are still against women's and LGBTIQA issues, and the people who are in charge of consitutional power in these countries are mostly male. So, in many ways the internet mirrors oppressive norms and ideas that have existed prior to its invention hence, it is for this reason that the inequality of access, participation and representation is replicating existing oppression and suppression of most notably, black women. Therefore, women ,and women in the global south specifically needs to be the vanguard of a new inclusive internet.
