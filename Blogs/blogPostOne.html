A reflection on the works of Borges- The Library of Babel and Garden of Forking Paths

In the book, The Library of Babel, Borges writes about a public space, known as the library. This universe of books is typified by identical hexagonal shaped rooms that are inhabited by books that are filled with all of the information that the universe has created as well as information that is yet to be put into the world. The library of babel has no limits, which is indicative of the way the internet structures information. The proliferation of the internet over past three decades has allowed many people across a broad range of demographics and geographies to partake in the knowledge formation and economy of what is shared on the internet.

The library of babel is exemplary of our ability to search for something on the internet. A user has no limits as to what they can search for because the internet has information that the universe has created, reference or adapted from traditional media, and information yet to be put into the word but put into the world at the point of searching or providing any input to the system. The library of babel signifies a public space that is unconstrained, therefore information might be very hard to get, thus it is interesting to consider Borges book, the Garden of Forking Paths to illustrate how scale can be mitigated by affectively managing 'time'.

It writes that time is an important factor to consider because time is a metaphor for diverging paths. Paths that exist simultaneously continuously intertwining and diverging all in one structure - the garden. So, this is indicative of how the browser as a structure can help us save time by creating tabs that can be made in the light that Borges suggests. Therefore this introduces the idea of hypertext and hyperlinking of information, which makes it easier to scale on information and save time while at it.

What does all of this have to do with my site?

The library of babel is reflected in this site through the way in which some of the images used for this site are linked from another website. The internet as a platform in this case is the library of babel and through the use of hyperlinks i am able to traverse the internet in an effort to gather information.
